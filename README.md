## Ⅰ. Executive Summary
**Yoga Fire** is a departure from the "Additive" paradigm of modern computer graphics. While traditional engines build scenes from hollow polygonal shells and "Generative AI" hallucinations flicker with temporal inconsistency, Yoga Fire utilizes a **Kiln** of deterministic, physically-grounded reality.

We begin with **Total Voxel Saturation**—a dense, high-entropy field of **Tessellated Gaussian Voxels (TGVs)**. Using **Deterministic Seeds**, we subtractively "chisel" matter into existence, ensuring that every point in space is governed by scale-coherent physical laws.

## Ⅱ. The Four Pillars

### 1. The Seed Schema (Material DNA)
Reality is germinated from **5KB Seed Manifests**. A Seed contains the "Genetic Code" of a material:
* **Mechanical:** Density ($\rho$), Young's Modulus ($E$), and Poisson's Ratio.
* **Optical:** Refractive Index ($n$) and Birefringence.
* **Acoustic:** Phonon base velocity and attenuation deltas.

### 2. The Strong Force (Neighbor-Aware Integrity)
In the Kiln, physics is not an "afterthought" or a plugin. Every voxel exists in a state of **Constant Handshake** with its neighbors. This "Strong Force" ensures that when one voxel is carved or moved, the surrounding lattice responds according to its Material DNA, maintaining manifold integrity across all scales.

### 3. Branched Flow (Emergent Optics)
We have bypassed the traditional "Render Loop." Light is treated as a persistent state of the grid. Using **Branched Flow**, photons propagate through the voxel field, "snagging" and refracting based on the local refractive index. The result is emergent caustics and internal reflections that are physically calculated, not artistically simulated.

### 4. Constant Acoustics (The Phonon Pulse)
Sound in Yoga Fire is a wave state propagating through the lattice. By utilizing the seed's stiffness and density, the Kiln calculates real-time vibration:
$$v_s = \sqrt{\frac{E}{\rho}}$$
Materials ring, thud, or crack based on their actual internal structure and inclusion phantasmagoria.

* **Core:** Written in **Rust**, compiled to **WebAssembly (Wasm)** for near-native, memory-safe execution in any modern browser.
